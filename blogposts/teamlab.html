Tokyo teamLab Borderless

The most beautiful digital art exhibit you'll ever visit

July 1, 2020

We'll be following a picture diary format since photos really do speak for themselves.

One my favorite rooms, it literally rains light

We visited teamLab Borderless, a digital art exhibit in Tokyo with art that flows from room to room, during the summer so it was pretty busy. It was by far one of my favorite places we visited in Tokyo (and all of Japan really) so feel free to dream with me while scrolling through these photos :)

Be sure to get there early, the lines can get long

It's a very big tourist attraction so if you have to bring your luggage (like us since this was our last day in Tokyo before heading to Kyoto), they have a luggage storage area with locks so it's very secure!

We entered through a sky bridge, ask for directions if you're lost!

Also, please note you need to buy tickets online, they do not sell tickets at all at the physical museum!

Roaming through rooms in no particular order :)
This room has a mirrored floor so they'll give you a wrap if you're wearing a dress!

The entire museum is 10,000 square meters, you could easily get lost wandering through all the peaceful light rooms.

If you stay long enough, you'll see the rainstorm!
There are floral bombs through all the hallways
Many walls are also mirrored for selfies :)
This room was a bit interesting, it's next to the Athletics Forest
This whole floor is like a huge trampoline, fyi!
There are many quieter rooms like this where you can relax
A little jungle park you can climb through while inside
Forest of Lamps - TOO BEAUTIFUL
The lamps constantly change colors but you can only stay in for up to 5 minutes sadly ):
Borderless World - literally a light waterfall
As you could guess, it gets crazy packed so be sure to book early tickets for the best pics/videos!
